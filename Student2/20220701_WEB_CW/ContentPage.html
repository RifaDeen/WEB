History

TIMELINE

1895: Birth of volleyball

While serving as the (Young Man's Christian Association) YMCA of Holyoke, Massachusetts's director of physical education, William G. Morgan created the game.
It was first known as "mintonette."

1900: New ball design

The use of a lighter, smaller ball improves the game's tactical and technical options.

1916: Offensive style showcase

The offensive style of setting and spiking is first showcased in the Philippines.

1947: Creation of FIVB

The Federation Internationale De Volley-Ball (FIVB) is created, paving the way for international competitions.

1964: Olympic debut

Volleyball makes its Olympic debut at Tokyo 1964.

1996: Beach volleyball at the olympics

Beach volleyball, which had its beginnings on Californian beaches in 1930, had to wait until 1996 for its acceptance on the Olympic program, and it has since expanded the sport's global appeal and popularity.

Late 1900s to present: Global growth and popularity

The expansion of international events like the FIVB World Championships, FIVB World League, FIVB World Grand Prix, FIVB World Cup, and FIVB Grand Champions Cup has enabled volleyball to have a particularly significant growth surge during the past two decades. Also, the Olympic Games, where both the indoor and outdoor competitions are now extremely popular.

Volleyball is now played by more than 800 million people globally, more than 120 years after it was first invented.
